This YouTube channel is populated with AI-generated visuals depicting gangs and rioters flooding every corner of New York City, evoking scenes reminiscent of The Walking Dead.

But the owner of this channel is Cash Jordan, a Manhattan-based real estate agent who is famous on social media for his open-house video.

Jordan started this channel as a way to attract renters during the height of the pandemic, when much of the in-person showings were paused.

But now, his channel has morphed to exclusively amplyfing misleading content about how migrants and homeless people are decimiating the city.

Ever since his pivot less than two years ago, Jordan has doubled his following to more than 1.2 million, a number that surpasses many local TV stations on YouTube, one of the most important sources for news in the US.

“Fundamentally, this is all about the algorithm,” said one internet scholar.
